About Me

I'm Nafisa Anjum, a Master's student at TU Dresden, with a background in machine learning, deep learning, and computer vision. My academic journey has involved practical work in natural language processing, digital pathology, and biomedical data analysis.

Currently, I'm working on my thesis at Katana Labs, focusing on the evaluation of foundation models for prostate cancer detection. Previously, I contributed to biomarker prediction research at Kather Lab using whole slide images. I've also developed a Napari plugin and contributed to open-source image analysis software.

Earlier in my career, I explored NLP during my Bachelor's, building systems to classify celebrities based on Twitter data using deep learning and sentiment analysis. I enjoy building interpretable and efficient AI tools that serve real-world healthcare and scientific needs.
